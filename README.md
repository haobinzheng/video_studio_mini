# FluxCut (iOS)

**FluxCut** is an iPhone app for building shareable videos from a script, photos, video clips, and music.

## World languages and voices

- **Many languages, one app.** You can work in the languages and writing systems that Apple’s on-device text-to-speech supports, including **English**, **Chinese (Simplified and Traditional)**, **Hindi and other Indic languages**, **Arabic**, and many more. Choose a **language** in the **Script** tab, then a **narration voice** that matches.
- **Voices come from the system** (Enhanced, Premium, Standard, etc.). You must download the voices you want on the device; FluxCut lists what the system exposes and offers **Reload iPhone Voices** after you add them. Siri-style personas in Settings (for example “Voice 1 / Voice 2”) are not available to third-party apps on iOS.

## Adding or downloading voices (iOS 26.3.1+)

1. On the iPhone, open the **Settings** app.
2. **Accessibility** → **VoiceOver** → **Speech**.
3. Add or download voices as shown there—for example the **Add Rotor Voice** (or similar) control on iOS 26.3.1, depending on region and exact OS build. Menu labels can vary slightly by iOS version and language.
4. Return to **FluxCut** and tap **Reload iPhone Voices** in the **Script** tab if the new voices do not appear at once.

If you still see a **Spoken Content** → **Voices** path on an older iOS, that can also be the place to download or manage speech voices on some devices.
